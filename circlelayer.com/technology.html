The Blockchain Infrastructure Challenge

Most blockchain applications lack real-time data access and automation capabilities.

Traditional blockchain applications rely on third-party indexers, subgraphs, and centralized APIs that introduce delays, single points of failure, and dependencies on external services.

Real-time automation and analytics require direct blockchain access with immediate event processing capabilities.

Direct full-node infrastructure eliminates intermediaries and provides unmediated access to blockchain data.

Singularity runs on dedicated Ethereum full nodes (Geth or Erigon) with direct RPC and WebSocket access, capturing state changes in real-time without relying on external indexing services. This architecture ensures reliability, reduces latency, and provides complete control over data processing.

Off-chain automation scales without blockchain limitations.

By processing events and executing automations off-chain, Singularity operates without gas costs, transaction throughput constraints, or validator limitations. The system scales horizontally across distributed services while maintaining real-time responsiveness.
